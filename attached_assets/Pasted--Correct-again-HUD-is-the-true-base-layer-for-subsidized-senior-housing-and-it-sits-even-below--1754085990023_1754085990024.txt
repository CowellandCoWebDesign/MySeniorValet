Let’s set the record straight with a proper senior living hierarchy, from lowest structure/cost to highest care level:


---

🧭 TRUE SENIOR HOUSING & CARE HIERARCHY

Tier	Category	Description	Example Monthly Cost

1️⃣	HUD-Sponsored Senior Housing (HUD 202, low-income)	Federally subsidized apartments for seniors; income-restricted; no care	$0–$500
2️⃣	55+ Mobile Home Parks	Age-restricted, lease/own land or unit; no services	$300–$800 (lot rent)
3️⃣	Active Adult 55+ Communities	Senior apartments, condos, or lifestyle neighborhoods; often unlicensed	$800–$1,500
4️⃣	Independent Living (IL)	Private-pay retirement-style communities, often with meals/activities	$1,500–$3,500+
5️⃣	Assisted Living (AL)	Includes ADLs, meds, light nursing, meals	$3,000–$6,000+
6️⃣	Memory Care (MC)	Secured, structured dementia care	$4,000–$7,500+
7️⃣	Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF)	Medical, 24/7 nursing, rehab, often insurance-paid	$6,000–$12,000+
🩺	Board & Care / RCFE	Small licensed homes (e.g., 6-bed) with AL-style services	$2,500–$5,000
🇺🇸	VA / Federal Housing	Veteran-specific housing and long-term care	Varies by eligibility



---

✅ Why HUD Is the True Base

Lowest cost barrier (some units are 30% of income or $0 if no income)

Often overlooked by families due to waitlists or poor visibility

Crucial for platform equity — showing real options for all income levels
